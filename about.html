Frank Zappa was a self taught musician,
composer, performer whose career spanned
over thirty years. In addition to being
member of the Mothers of Invention, who as
a collaborative group released over 60 albums
he was also a solo artist producing many unique
works of his own. His genre of music really could
not be classified or put into a box becuase it
encompassed so many different styles of music and
had a feel and sound that was entirely its own thing.
The Mothers of Invention debuted in 1966 with their
first album titled "Freak Out!" His musical style and
incorporation of political and societal satire led
to him being referred to as the "godfather" of
comedy rock. In addition to the incorporation of
political ideologies in his music he also held strong
opinions regarding many different matters that were
famously controversial during the 1960s and 1970s,
including the War on Drugs and media censorship.
Despite not being a drug user himself, and in fact
strongly dissapproving of drug use he vouched for the
decriminalization and regulation of substances
believing it would lessen the impact that they
had on American society. Zappa was also and atheist
raised in a religious family and generally rejected
Christian values and had a strong distaste against
organized religion. Rather than being a figurehead
and a negative spectacle in the public eye
like many other "rock" artists of the time Zappa
established himself as a multi-layered
person with insightful views on the functions
of society and how people would compose themselves
during his lifetime. He truly stood out among the
crowd and was not afraid to express his thoughts
and opinions that would be considered highly
controversial during the era of his fame through
his music, which was so well executed.
Jumping way forward, in 1993 Frank Zappa
contracted prostate cancers and sadly passed away
shortly before his 53rd birthday.
He was a legendary musician who created
masterful works of art and was an inspiration
to many artists after him. He truly stood out
and no one can quite match his musical talent
and his seemingly inherent ability to craft notes
into something mind-blowing and unique.
